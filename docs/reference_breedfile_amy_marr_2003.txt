Here are some descriptions of the breed file we will use for the book,
please read below carefully to understand the file!
The file has 2517 datalines and a row of titles 


Columns which are not named in an obvious way include:

nestrec=nest record, don't worry about this column, it has to do with
making it easy to track the original way the data was once upon a time
entered, don't use it  (it is a confusing column because 3 original nests
had to be removed from the dataset because Jamie figured out that they
never existed except as placeholders when an assistant was entering data,
thus there are 2520 original nest records but really only 2517 nests in the
files now, I really needed a way to flag these missing lines when working
with older datasets, sound confusing?, don't use the row and you won't have
a problem!)

terr= territory of nest location in most years, let me know if you need all
years as it is not complete for some years but I can enter the data in a
few hours if needed

maleage = age of male
maleatt = male's attempt number
femage = age of female
fematt = female's attempt number
nestx = x coordinate of nest
nesty = y coordinate of nest, no one has ever really proofed or worked with
coordinate data so beware of errors

dfe=. in this column if it could not be estimated because
1. it was found well after failure and there was really no way to estimate
when it happened within plus or minus a week
2. it was found empty and observer guessed it was maybe never actually used
so there would be no dfe for that attempt (most common scenario)
3. data was just too poor or confusing to estimate the dfe properly (a real
effort was made not to allow this except with 1975 because that was just a
big mess and I couldn't make sense of it)

stagfind = stage of find
NB=nestbuilding
MTD=found empty nest, shows signs it once had eggs
MTUK = found empty nest, nest either never used or already failed after use
EL = egg laying
EAF = eggs or shells present in nest but nest found after fail
IC = incubating
HY = hatched young
FY = fledged young
NFN = never found nest
UK = unknown (observations too confusing to assign stage of find)

eggslaid = were eggs laid in this nest?
Y = yes
U = unknown
N = no

wholeclutch = was the entire clutch observed?
Y = yes, bird seen incubating the nest
N = no or can't be certain because bird was not seen INCUBATING the nest

expt= experiment codes:
1=1975 brood swap
2=1979 mate removal
3=1979 feeding at start of season or close neighbor and with likely access,
VERY IMPORTANT TO EXCLUDE
4.1=1985 feeding expt neighbor, VERY IMPORTANT TO EXCLUDE 
4.2=1985 feeding expt fed bird, VERY IMPORTANT TO EXCLUDE 
5=1986 cross foster
6=1988 Wes's feeding expt birds or close neighbor with likely access, VERY
IMPORTANT TO EXCLUDE
7=1996 Rothstein egg expt, not sure if this affected nest success
8=1997,1998 3 nest attempts were disrupted by temperature probe expt,
female probably abandoned due to temperature probe, other birds were not
coded because they were not influenced

origdfe=dfe in original breed file, the other dfe column (first in file)
includes ones that I estimated within a week, do not use this data unless
to understand why some analysis may have changed slightly from something
that was previously observed, this was necessary because dfe has not been
entered consistently thru time, some observers required a much higher level
of certainty than others and Peter and Jamie wanted certainty within a week 

filenote='PB' if nest was only partly built, these should NOT be counted as
attempts for cumulative counts because they were only entered in the
database in some years


The file was created from the usual old breed file with a backfill routine
and weeks of dataproofing.  If indep is a number and fledge is unknown then
fledge equals indep, fledge is a number and band is unknown then band
equals fledge, etc.  Why?  Two reasons:  First, this was done in most early
years anyway! Second, I added the stagfind column so you can AND should
eliminate data based on stage of find.  By backfilling, the dataset is much
more consistent across years.  

If there were no eggs or hatchlings recorded for a nest, I actually went
back to the cards and entered minimum counts of what was observed.  You can
tell if the whole clutch size was observed from the wholeclutch column.
This was important because there is info in partial clutches that was being
lost in important ways in some years but not others thus creating problems
in all sorts of analyses.  In particular, many nests found during
nestbuilding or laying fail in cowbird years but they might get eliminated
in some analyses if the observer in that year did not enter a clutch size
that was partially complete.  


The failcode categories are as follows.
1	Mouse shit
2	Shell remains or evidence of yolk
3	Egg punctured but not eaten, kicked egg outside nest
4	Lining disturbed or pulled 
5	Nest tilted
6	Nest overturned/ demolished/ gone
7	Vegetation parted or trampled
8	Female died
9	Abandoned, all eggs present
10	Abandoned, egg loss
11	Sparrow eggs replaced with cowbird eggs and nest abandoned
12	Empty intact, no signs of disturbance
13	Mouse shit + egg shells
14	Mouse shit + lining disturbed
15	Egg shells + lining disturbed
16	Lining pulled + nest tilted
17	Lining pulled + veg parted
18	Young beaten/ hole in head/ broken legs in nest, chick dead or alive
outside of nest prior to fledge age but not eaten by the predator
19	Young starved, intact but dead in nest
20	Legs or wings of nestlings found in or near nest
21	Eggs remain in nest but hatched young gone, no signs 
22	Human accident/ experiment
23	Other
24	Success
